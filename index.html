The frame

Which layer does each part of a case actually belong on

Most of what sits on a case file is traditional code and database work: intake forms, document checklists, deadline math, status routing. A good chunk is rule-based logic: category-specific evidence thresholds, form package validation, adjudication-timeline heuristics. A narrow slice is real AI territory: drafting petition narratives, spotting weak evidence before an attorney burns an hour on it, answering the client at 11pm with something accurate enough to hold until the morning. Pushing AI into the first two layers is where margin goes to die. Pulling AI back into the third is where attorneys get their afternoons back.

We would not come in and rebuild your stack. We would look at one case, end to end, and map every hand-off between the portal, the AI intake, the paralegal pass, the senior attorney review, and the client. Then we would tell you which hand-offs belong in code, which belong in rules, which belong in AI, and which belong in a single attorney's judgment. That is the orchestration layer. It sits on top of what you already have.
